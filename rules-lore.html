Lore Lens

The site does not rewrite official history. Instead, it uses comic tension, archival motifs, and patriotic language to dramatize a beloved strategy game.

Core Understanding

Game of the Generals is a duel of hidden information, nerve, and memory.

Objective

Win by capturing the enemy flag or escorting your own flag to the opponent's back rank without interception.

Hidden Ranks

Each piece carries a secret identity. Players infer strength from movement, sacrifice patterns, and psychological rhythm.

Resolution

When units meet, ranks are revealed and compared. Higher ranks prevail, except for special counters like the spy and private.

Character

The game feels military in structure but theatrical in practice. Bluffing becomes story, and every reveal becomes a panel turn.

Doctrine File

Expandable rules notes

How the opening arrangement shapes the entire match

Initial placement determines whether your army looks aggressive, cautious, or misleading. The most successful lineups conceal intent while preserving response options for the flag, spy, and high officers.

Why deduction matters more than brute force

Because ranks remain hidden until contact, the strongest player is not always the one with boldest attacks. The stronger player is the one who notices patterns, preserves uncertainty, and times reveals with discipline.

The cultural tone behind this site's patriotic framing

"Pagkamakabayan" here signals respect, duty, and collective memory. The site leans into civic atmosphere without claiming to be an official historical document or teaching aid.

How the comic-book art direction supports the rules

Heavy borders, caption boxes, textured gradients, and dramatic shadows make hidden-information play feel serialized and mythic. The UI becomes a stage where inference reads like a cliffhanger.
